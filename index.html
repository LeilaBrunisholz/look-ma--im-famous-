ABRAHAM LINCOLN

Abraham Lincoln (1809â€“1865) was the 16th President of the United States, known for his leadership during the Civil War and his commitment to ending slavery. Born in a log cabin, Lincoln's self-education propelled him into politics, where he served in the Illinois State Legislature and the U.S. House of Representatives. His presidency, from 1861 to 1865, marked a crucial period in American history. The Emancipation Proclamation and the Gettysburg Address are enduring testaments to his legacy. Tragically, Lincoln was assassinated in 1865, leaving an indelible mark on the nation.
